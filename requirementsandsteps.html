STEPS AND REQUIREMENTS

A Guide to Properly Submitting Your Vote and the Necessary Requirements for Voting

STEP BY STEP GUIDE

  1. Proceed to the Voter's Assistance Desk (VAD). Here, you can get your precinct, queue and room number.
  2. Go to your assigned room. Present yourself to the Electoral Board in your designated voting room. State your name, precinct number and sequence number.
  3. Take a ballot. An official will hand you a ballot secrecy folder and a marking pen.
  4. Fill in the ballot. Fully shade the candidate ovals so that the machine can read your vote. Remember not to over-vote.
  5. Insert your ballot in the Vote Counting Machine (VCM). Stand by and wait for the machine to process your vote. Do not leave before this is completed.
  6. Check your voter's receipt. Were your votes processed correctly? If yes, drop the receipt in the provided receptacle. Otherwise, consult the board of elections inspectors and register your complaint.
  7. Present your forefinger nail for staining. The poll clerk will stain your nail with indelible ink as a mark that you've voted.

WHAT TO BRING WHEN VOTING?

  1. Valid ID: A government-issued ID such as a driver's license, passport, or postal ID to confirm your identity and eligibility to vote.

  2. Voter's Registration Form

    : If required, bring your voter's registration card or any proof of your voter registration.

  3. Ballot Pen or Marker

    : Some precincts might provide a pen, but it's better to bring your own to avoid any delays or issues.

  4. Proof of Address

    : If you're voting in a new area or have changed your address, some precincts may ask for a proof of your current residence.

  5. Positive Attitude and Patience

    : Don't forget to bring your willingness to participate and your patience as lines can be long.
